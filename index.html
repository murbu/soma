Soma Water Filtration utilizes the power of mushroom mycofiltration to remediate polluted water sources affected by stormwater runoff.



Explore What We Do

-Problem-

The use of synthetic fertilizers, fossil fuels, and untreated sewage pollutes waterways, threatening wildlife, soil ecosystems, and human health through runoff and point-source pollution from agricultural practices, leading to issues like soil erosion, habitat loss, eutrophication, and unsafe drinking water.
-Solution-

Mycelium, with its ability to absorb oils, heavy metals, and excess nutrients, can be used in layered mats to filter contaminants from water, improving water quality and environmental health through the action of its fine hyphae.
-Market Appeal-
In Washington, where agriculture dominates land use and contributes to over 2,000 polluted water bodies, demand for local water filtration systems is driven by state regulation requiring landowners to manage runoff and by growing public interest in conservation, highlighting a significant market potential for innovative solutions like personal mycelium gardens for prevention and remediation.

Our Mission

Our goal is to simplify a way for the public to contribute to the process of remediating polluted bodies of water and improve a regional capacity to filter water.
